Context of Discovery

The Cult Relief of Gad was discovered alongside the other relief dedicated by Hairan beneath the debris of the upper walls and ceiling in naos 3 of the Temple of Gadde. This structure was excavated in section H, block 1 by the Yale-French team during their 7th and 8th seasons between 1933 and 1935. The reliefs were found broken, with the cult relief of Gad being in 32 pieces. A third relief was also partially recovered from this context, with 9 pieces extracted from the rubble. C. C. Torrey speculates that this last relief may have been more intact that the others and therefore removed by earlier looters at the site. It is likely to have depicted either Iarhibol or Malakbel, other Palmyrene gods. All three seem to have been made in a similar style, implying that the three originally went together, occupying the niches in the back wall of the naos.
