Context of Discovery

The votive relief of the goddess Nemesis was found on May 8th 1928 (the very end of the first season of excavation) at the Palmyrene Gate (the main gate in the Western wall of the city) near the third bay. When it was discovered, the relief was broken in two pieces; these have been subsequently mended. There is also evidence that the relief was broken and repaired in antiquity. The faces of all figures, including the griffin at Nemesis’ feet, have been smashed, apparently deliberately.
